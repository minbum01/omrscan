2. Key Insight: qMin is Dead Weight

qMin is almost always 0.000 for elongated bubbles

In circular bubbles, qMin (quarter-minimum) measures how evenly the ink is distributed across quadrants. For elongated (vertically stretched) bubbles, the aspect ratio causes most quadrant-based measurements to fail, resulting in qMin = 0.000 for nearly all choices (both marked and unmarked).

This means 25% of the composite score (Formula E) is wasted on a feature that provides zero discriminative power.

What Makes Elongated Bubbles Different from Circular?

Elongated (h/w > 1.4)

  • qMin is nearly always 0.000 (useless feature)
  • f (fill ratio) values are generally lower overall
  • e (edge/excess) values are also lower
  • s (pixel sum) varies widely due to aspect ratio
  • Gap between marked/unmarked is smaller
  • Higher false-null rate with standard thresholds

Circular (h/w ~ 1.0)

  • qMin is meaningful (0.0 ~ 0.8+ range)
  • f values show clear marked/unmarked separation
  • e values also show good separation
  • s is more consistent
  • Gap is larger, easier to detect
  • Formula E works well
